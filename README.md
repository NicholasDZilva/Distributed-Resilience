# Distributed Resilience  
### A Structural Response to Infrastructure Cascade Scenarios

## Purpose

This repository presents a practical, non-coercive framework for maintaining societal continuity during large-scale infrastructure disruptions (e.g. payment network failures, authentication outages, supply chain breakdowns).

The framework is designed to:
- Preserve transactional capacity without requiring centralized digital mandates
- Maintain trust, agency, and legitimacy during crisis conditions
- Reduce systemic fragility caused by tightly coupled digital dependencies
- Integrate simulations as advisory inputs rather than authoritative justifications

This work does **not** oppose preparedness modeling, simulation, or coordination.  
It focuses on **structural resilience**, **contestability**, and **distributed agency**.

---

## Core Design Principles

1. **Resilience without coercion**  
   Systems must function even when central authority, connectivity, or compliance mechanisms fail.

2. **Simulations inform, humans decide**  
   Population-scale simulations are treated as advisory tools, not policy engines.

3. **Decentralized continuity over centralized optimization**  
   Local capacity and trust-aware coordination reduce cascade risk more effectively than single-point control.

4. **Auditability by architecture**  
   Decisions, metrics, and triggers are observable and reviewable without requiring real-time disclosure of sensitive details.

5. **Automatic reintegration**  
   All decentralized mechanisms are temporary, self-terminating, and designed to phase out as infrastructure recovers.

## What This Repository Contains

- A three-part framework covering:
  - Structural threats and failure modes
  - Decentralized response architecture
  - Policy interface and simulation-to-action translation
- Operational components such as:
  - Local Trade Voucher systems
  - Decentralized verification mechanisms
  - Trust-aware metrics (CIAT, PTI, SAR)
  - Distributed activation thresholds
- Implementation considerations, stress tests, and recovery protocols

This is a **reference architecture**, not a turnkey product.
